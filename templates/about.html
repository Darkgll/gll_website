About Me

In a few words.

In 2019 I have graduated from the Financial University under the Government of the Russian Federation (Barnaul Branch). After graduating, I was invited to work in a new startup project for the development of an educational cryptocurrency online exchange. In time, I realized, that this work will not give me a good future. Due to the pandemic, the project was closed. After this I decided to search for what I really want to do in my life. I wanted to do something interesting, something that can be really beneficial for people, not only for myself, and at the end of year 2020, I have made a very important decision to learn programming.

On December 2021 I've got my first programmer job in the company Sovcombank Technologies, as a Python developer (Specialist). Before that time I knew nothing about programming.

My current job mostly includes:

  • Getting data from by API or from local files, parsing it and saving to database (SQL Oracle, sqlalchemy)
  • Automation processing of tasks in "Pyrus", like: creating tasks, updating tasks, saving data from tasks to DB, writing comments and else.
  • Making notification bots, services for Telegram groups, email notifications.
  • Made various important bots, that are working with Active Directory, creating, updating and deleting Users, by getting info from tasks (by using LDAP library).
  • Made small service for storing cash in Redis (Using FastAPI, SQLAlchemy).

In free time at home learning, making myself familiar with:

  • JavaScript, Node.js React (took a course at Udemy to be able to understand core of web development).
  • At first year of learning programming, (by learning Python) made a website on Flask with help of the course from Udemy.
  • Django (Learning it now, by CS50, various courses and docs)
  • Helping my friend who is studying at University, to learn programming (C++, C#, Java, Python. Even if I never worked with Java, for example, it is much easier for me to understand basics, so I can explain it to my friend. While looking at his tasks, I'm also learning basics of these languages by myself and getting a good practice.
  • Slowly learning Japanese (by Anki, YouTube, a with help of my wonderfull Japanese friend).

My dream is what I live for.

I've started my programming path to be able to find a job in Japan later. To be a valuable employee there. To make something good for Japan and for the World, if it would be possible. That's wonderfull thing, that without any doubts, my decision to become a programmer was the best decision in my life.

And I'm still working on my dream and I will never stop.
